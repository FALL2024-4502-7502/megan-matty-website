The Best Marvel Cinematic Movies

Nobody does superhero movies better than the MCU. These are some of the best (in my opinion).

Avengers: Endgame

Directed by Anthony and Joe Russo

This MCU movie will always remain one of my top movies of all time. The fact that it was able to cover so many different story lines with so many different characters and to do it in a way that makes sense is a such a huge feat. “Cap on your left,” will never not give me chills. Image Credits: Disney

Captain America: The Winter Soldier

Directed by Anthony and Joe Russo

Introducing Anthony Mackie and Sebastian Stan to the MCU in these roles was a true gift. Bucky Barnes being the villain of this movie, after last seeing him fall off the train in ‘Captain America: The First Avenger’ was mind-blowing at the time. I remember seeing this movie in theaters with my friends and when Bucky’s mask fell off and Steve Rogers goes “Bucky?” we went absolutely crazy. Image Credits: Disney

Black Panther

Directed by Ryan Coogler

Director Ryan Coogler created a thoughtful and engaging experience that included not only breathtaking visuals, but amazing characters as well. At the end of the movie, I hoped that somehow Michael B. Jordan’s character would have made it through, but alas he did not. Backed by a musical soundtrack that, in my opinion, has no skips is just the cherry on top of this wonderful movie. Completely unrelated to the first Black Panther movie, Angela Basset should have won the Oscar for ‘Best Supporting Actress’ for her role in Black Panther: Wakanda Forever. I’m sorry Jaimie Lee Curtis, she gave a much better performance. Image Credits: Disney

Spider-Man: No Way Home

Directed by Jon Watts

Like other movies I have included on this lest, this one is also a movie that I’ll never forget seeing in theaters. The way the entire theater screamed when Andrew Garfield popped up. And then minutes later, when Toby McGuire popped up, crazy. Hats off to Marvel for being able to contain a huge secret like that. The MCU once again nails it on the head with the crossover of multiple heroes and characters that are beloved without it being too much for the audience. Image Credits: Disney

Thor: Ragnarok

Directed by Taika Waititi

Taika Waititi brought such life to this movie. This movie could have followed suit of the two other Thor centered movies and been on the more serious side. Waititi politely said “No thanks”. And would go on to make this bro comedy. And it was nice to see Thor and Loki’s relationship as brothers continue to grow. And Cate Blanchet proves to be a wonderful villain in Hela. Image Credits: Disney

Guardians of the Galaxy

Directed by James Gunn

Going into this movie, I had no clue who any of these characters were. And after seeing it can’t imagine and MCU where Rocket and Groot aren’t best friends. Chris Pratt gives a great performance as Peter Quill, a lovable idiot who was abducted and raised by aliens as a child. The movie also wouldn’t be the same without its killer soundtrack. The sound track included major bops like “Hooked on a Feeling” by Blue Swede and “Ain’t No Mountain High Enough” by Marvin Gaye. A dance off to save the universe is not how I thought this movie would end. Image Credits: Disney
